Treasury Is Your Company's Most Underappreciated Input.

For treasury managers, it's helpful to begin your career in banking, it will provide a fantastic foundation of financial education that has had a direct influence on all of the work I do now. Such teams are crucial to the success of their organizations for reasons that I will explain in this article about treasury management best practices, but they are frequently misunderstood or underestimated. I've had to explain (courteously) that corporate treasury isn't all about wooden chests or whip-rounds for group holiday cash on a regular basis. This DCOSS article will give you a little insight into what treasury management is and the things you need to know about the field

What Is Treasury Management?

          The treasury management team of a company or corporate business is responsible for managing and strategizing cash inputs and outputs at both the business development level and strategic level. In addition the Treasury function may also have an asset liability management (ALM) desk that manages the risk of interest rate mismatch and liquidity; and a funds transfer pricing or pooling function that prices liquidity for business lines (asset sales teams) within the organization. Note that Management (or managing) is the administration of an organization, whether it is a business, a non-profit organization, or a government body. It is the art and science of managing resources of the business. Management involves identifying the mission, objective, procedures, rules and manipulation of the human capital of an enterprise to contribute to the success of the enterprise. Scholars have focused on the management of individual, organizational, and inter-organizational relationships. This implies effective communication: an enterprise environment (as opposed to a physical or mechanical mechanism) implies human motivation and implies some sort of successful progress or system outcome. As such, management is not the manipulation of a mechanism (machine or automated program), not the herding of animals, and can occur either in a legal or in an illegal enterprise or environment. From an individual's perspective, management does not need to be seen solely from an enterprise point of view, because management is an essential function in improving one's life and relationships both formal an informal. Management is therefore everywhere and it has a wider range of application. Communication and a positive endeavor are two main aspects of it either through enterprise or through independent pursuit. Plans, measurements, motivational psychological tools, goals, and economic measures (profit, etc.) may or may not be necessary components for there to be management. So now we know what management and treasury management are. Inputs and outputs of business and corporate treasury are controlled by treasury managers or firms through:

  1. The amount held and
  2. Its liquidity.

          The sheer size of the balance sheet and the relative stickiness (liquidity) of assets and liabilities held are the two levers. The fundamentals of an organization are enabled by their management of this: allowing teams to operate and conduct activities by ensuring that there is cash on hand, whether in the petty cash box or an opportunistic M&A raid. Treasuries participate in the macro-financial direction of a company and oversee the execution of company-wide strategies, in addition to enabling business-as-usual (BAU) activities. For example, if the board decides to buy a company or expand into new markets, Treasury will assist in determining the company's fit on the balance sheet and ultimately finding the cash (or issuing stock) to purchase it.

The responsibilities and benefits of treasury management firms such as DCOSS Associates to your business

portfolio management

Treasuries are financial asset managers for their company, investing spare cash that sits on the balance sheet to generate a return (and thus, lower FTP). This is often a very creative exercise that involves the search for yield, liquidity, and capital efficiency. Braeburn Capital, for example, is the asset management arm of Apple, a company that regularly has reserve treasury funds of over $200 billion!

asset liability management

Asset liability management is concerned with the mix of assets and liabilities on a balance sheet and the resulting mismatches in tenor, currency, and interest rate (cost). Companies have a variety of instruments on their balance sheets that behave differently. The way they interact with one another and represent the overall position is analogous to the concept of Beta in portfolio management. Asset liability management is especially important for bank treasury management because their primary purpose is based on the gearing dynamic of borrowing and lending money.

Companies have a natural tendency to stretch this funding mismatch to its limit because it is generally cheaper to borrow short-term liabilities and invest in long-term assets. This can all come crashing down during market flashpoints when credit becomes scarce and liabilities become more difficult to roll over. This liquidity horizon is monitored by an asset management function, which prescribes limit buffers and advises on any changes that can be observed in advance. Proactively optimizing assets and liabilities increases profitability and business opportunities.

trading and hedging

The treasury function is in charge of hedging company-wide interest rate and currency risk, and will use derivatives to balance the books. These risk management strategies can range from FX spot trades to long-term interest rate swaps, depending on the sophistication of the business. A sudden change in currency would distort the risk in terms of balance-sheet proportions and deal profitability. In order to maintain parity, trading cross-currency swap derivatives to solidify the asset positions into GBP would be a smart move.

Treasury Management Best Practices

Building and managing a capable team for the benefit of the entire business, with the ultimate goal of managing the firm's liquidity and mitigating operational, financial, and reputational risk. The best practices listed below should always be at the forefront of any treasury management and strategy team.

Agency incentive cost

From a performance standpoint, it is critical to get compensation and incentives right. This is visible in how the team is formed and literally in where they are seated in the office. Treasuries interact with external parties and must thus project confidence and the corporate image. There may be perverse incentives because the team is not a profit center (profits flow to the central company entity). Outside of severe market stress, for example, taking an ultra-risk policy of raising long-dated cash and lending it out short term is not a commercially sound practice. However, if the treasury team is not incentivized, they may choose this option because it is safe and they will still be paid. Establishing suitable and compelling incentives for treasury staff reduces agency costs. It is an empirical fact that variable compensation related to fund tranfer pricing movement is an interesting tool for measuring holistic team performance.

Effective Communication

Treasury management, as the company's ears to the financial markets and balance sheet straddlers, is an important news source. It should convert macroeconomic events into risks or, conversely, opportunities. Rather than disseminating information, such information should be packaged as actionable insights: "How does this affect our company?" Reporting a company's cash position is an important end-of-day reporting task, but it should not stop there. Reporting to the executive committee should be done concisely, rather than as a dump of mundane reports.

Responsibilties and boundaries

The investment portfolios that treasuries oversee are comparable to the spare change you store in a jar by your front door. This money is the unutilized cash that is daily left in the pile. It is on fire and might be used the following day. The yield obtained from investing it, however, reduces the dead-weight loss of undeployed money and can have positive effects on tangential hedging and counterparty relationships. Liquidity, risk, and capital efficiency are the three cornerstones of treasury portfolio management. Although some may find these conservative aims uninteresting (note the lack of yield), they differ from those of the majority of other portfolio managers. However, it gets rather intriguing when you consider how you might use certain instruments in cash management methods. The fourth option is yield, however in my opinion there is no opportunity cost to not investing, therefore any yield that is good is a success right away. The key is that although London Whale London Whale, who lost $6.2 billion, will be remembered by all, including the press, no one will remember the Treasurer, who gained a meager 50bp on their investment portfolio. Therefore, avoid making a hero out of yourself when managing the liquidity portfolio's funds.

Build and deploy enterprise resource planning (ERP)

ERP is a software component of treasury management that automates the repetitive steps needed to manage a company's cash flow. Without effective software to splice the balance sheet and communicate liquidity positions and risk exposures, treasurers are blind. Money moves by the second, and if you don't keep up, you'll be chasing shadows while trying to reconcile positions. Treasuries require systems that can account for a wide range of functionality; the fewer systems, the better for ensuring smooth workflow crossover.

Internal leadership structures

A treasury function can emphasize asset and liability focused teams, acting as a liaison between the two. In banks, this is the deposit takers and lenders, whereas in a corporation, it could be the property team versus the payables department. Being objective and involved with the business units, as well as providing solutions to roadblocks, will ultimately benefit the organization as a whole and increase the company's financial positions and future.

compare prices and strategies

Treasuries are buy-side institutions that rely on market makers for financial products ranging from simple deposit accounts to complex derivatives. The most common mistake I see here is when a team does not properly shop around and has a diverse bench of liquidity providers. Typically, the dealer will stick to a few providers, either because they are not proactive enough or because they enjoy the client entertainment that their wide spreads unwittingly pay for. In addition to the foregoing, a treasury should interact directly with the market. Going through other teams within the same company is a P&L pass the parcel exercise that breeds inefficiency. Similar to how a sales-driven SaaS firm would handle external clients, an efficient treasury should do the same. Keep a CRM updated with links to banks, brokers, and funds along with rankings of their benefits and drawbacks. This will guarantee that pricing is competitive and that a wide phonebook of liquidity providers is available in case markets turn bad. Electronic trading systems are excellent for obtaining accurate price comparisons, saving time, and assessing execution quality.
